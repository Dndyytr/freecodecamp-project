Linus Torvalds

The man who created the Linux kernel and Git

Here's a time line of Linus Torvalds' life:

  • 1969 - Born in Helsinki, Finland
  • 1991 - While studying computer science at the University of Helsinki, begins developing his own operating system kernel as a free alternative to MINIX, later naming it Linux. Announces it on a newsgroup with the now-famous post: "I'm doing a (free) operating system (just a hobby, won't be big and professional like GNU)."
  • 1992 - Linux kernel version 0.12 is released under the GNU General Public License (GPL), transforming it into a collaborative open-source project that rapidly gains global developer support.
  • 1997 - Marries Tove Torvalds, a former Finnish karate champion, with whom he has three daughters.
  • 2005 - After a dispute over licensing with a commercial source control system, develops Git in just a few days to manage Linux kernel development. It becomes the world's most widely used version control system.
  • 2008 - Releases the first version of the Linux kernel 2.6.28, which includes major improvements in performance, power management, and device support, solidifying Linux's role in servers, supercomputers, and mobile devices.
  • 2011 - Receives the Millennium Technology Prize, one of the world's largest technology awards, for his creation of Linux, cited as enabling "a new era of open collaboration."
  • 2012 - Linux powers over 90% of the world’s supercomputers and the vast majority of web servers. Android, based on the Linux kernel, becomes the most popular mobile operating system globally.
  • 2014 - After years of using the Linux kernel for internal development, Tesla announces that its vehicles run on a customized version of Linux, highlighting its versatility beyond traditional computing.
  • 2018 - Steps back temporarily from Linux kernel development to address his communication style, then returns with a new code of conduct for the open-source community emphasizing respect and professionalism.
  • 2021 - Moves from the Open Source Development Lab to work under the umbrella of the Linux Foundation, continuing to oversee the development of the Linux kernel.
  • Present - Continues to live in the United States with his family and remains the final authority on what code is merged into the mainline Linux kernel, maintaining a pivotal role in shaping modern computing.

"One of the most important things in life is to have fun, and if you're not having fun, you're not doing it right. That's how I wrote Linux — because it was fun."

-- Linus Torvalds
